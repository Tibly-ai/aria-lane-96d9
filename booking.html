Before You Ask

Common booking questions

The things organizers most often want to know before hitting send.

Do you bring your own sound equipment?

Yes. For solo and duo bookings I supply a full professional PA suitable for up to 200 guests. For larger events and full-band sets, I'll coordinate directly with your venue or provide a tech rider for their sound team.

Can you learn a specific song for us?

Absolutely — it's one of my favorite parts of the job. Up to three custom song requests are included with every wedding and private party booking. Just give me at least three weeks' notice.

How far will you travel?

Anywhere in the country for the right event. Performances within 60 miles of Portland carry no travel fee; beyond that, travel and accommodation are quoted transparently up front — no surprises.

What does a booking cost?

It depends on lineup, length, and location — a solo ceremony set is priced very differently from a full-band evening. Every quote is itemized and obligation-free, so the easiest way to find out is to ask.

What happens if plans change?

Life happens. Deposits are transferable to a new date within twelve months at no charge, subject to availability. Full cancellation terms are spelled out plainly in the booking agreement.

Can we hear you before we commit?

Of course. Head to my portfolio for live recordings and videos, and if I'm playing a public venue near you, I'm always glad to share upcoming show dates.
